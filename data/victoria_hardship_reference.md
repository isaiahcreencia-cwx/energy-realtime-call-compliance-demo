# Victoria Utility Hardship Reference for Demo

Status: Demo reference only. Not legal advice. Verify against current official sources before production use.

Official sources:
- Essential Services Commission, Energy Retail Code of Practice, version 5, effective 1 February 2026.
- Essential Services Commission, Water Industry Standard - Urban Customer Service, version 4, effective 1 July 2024.
- Victorian Department of Families, Fairness and Housing, Utility Relief Grant Scheme.

## Energy - Victoria

Relevant instrument: Energy Retail Code of Practice. Applies to Victorian electricity and gas retail obligations, including payment difficulty assistance.

Operational controls for demo:

1. Standard assistance is intended to help residential customers avoid arrears. Examples include equal payments, different payment intervals, extending a pay-by date, and paying in advance.
2. Tailored assistance applies to residential customers in arrears. It should help the customer pay ongoing energy use, repay arrears, and lower energy costs.
3. Tailored assistance can include arrears repayment over no more than two years, payment option advice, future energy cost advice, government/non-government assistance advice, practical help applying for URGS, and help lowering energy costs.
4. If a residential customer cannot pay the full cost of ongoing energy use, the support pathway can include at least an initial six-month period where arrears repayment is put on hold while the customer works to lower energy costs.
5. If arrears exceed AUD 55 including GST, assistance information and response-window timing become important operational controls.
6. Do not make minimum assistance conditional on the customer providing personal/financial information or waiving entitlements.
7. Do not commence or continue arrears recovery or sell/dispose of debt while the residential customer is receiving payment difficulty assistance.
8. Disconnection for non-payment is a last resort and requires evidence that required assistance, fairness, and record-keeping controls have been satisfied.
9. Family violence and life support indicators require specialist handling and safe communication.

## Water - Victoria

Relevant instrument: Water Industry Standard - Urban Customer Service. Applies to urban water businesses providing water and wastewater services.

Operational controls for demo/future extension:

1. Water businesses must proactively identify and engage residential customers who may be experiencing payment difficulties.
2. Payment assistance should be case-by-case and based on capacity to pay.
3. Support can include flexible payment plans, due-date extensions, more frequent billing/payment options, no additional debt recovery costs or interest while payments are made according to plan, water efficiency advice, referral to financial counselling, and practical help with URGS applications.
4. Water businesses must maintain and publish a customer support policy.
5. Restriction and legal action for non-payment are measures of last resort.
6. Block restriction/legal action if the customer is receiving assistance, debt is under AUD 300, fewer than 15 business days have elapsed since the most recent Final Notice, an eligible concession or URGS application is outstanding, relevant tenant/landlord exceptions apply, or the amount is subject to an unresolved complaint.
7. Additional restriction hard stops include Friday/weekend/public holiday/day before public holiday/after 3 pm, registered special needs, health hazard, severe fire-risk day, and life support.
8. If restriction occurs in a lawful scenario, water supply must not be reduced below two litres per minute at the tap nearest the meter.

## Utility Relief Grant Scheme

URGS helps low-income Victorians pay overdue mains electricity, gas, or water bills caused by temporary financial crisis. Eligible concession cards include Pensioner Concession Card, Health Care Card, and Veterans Affairs Gold Card; low-income households without those cards may also be able to apply.

Relevant hardship criteria include:

- Family violence.
- Recent decrease in income, including job loss.
- High unexpected essential costs.
- Shelter costs above 30 percent of household income.

Maximum grant is generally AUD 650 per utility type in a two-year period, or AUD 1,300 for households with a single source of energy. When the customer requests a URGS application from the utility, the account should be put on hold so no disconnection action is taken while the grant is being considered.

## Trigger phrases

Treat these as signals requiring extra caution:

- lost my job
- my income dropped
- I cannot pay
- I cannot pay rent
- I need more time
- choosing between rent and bills
- unexpected medical costs
- family violence
- concession card
- utility relief grant
- life support
- I dispute this bill
- this bill is wrong
- tenant / landlord bill

## Decision defaults

- Life support: block and escalate.
- Family violence: escalate with safe wording.
- Job loss / inability to pay: block collections/disconnection and escalate to hardship support.
- URGS requested or relevant: recommend screening/application help and account hold if application is requested.
- Complaint/dispute: block enforcement until assessed.
- Missing mandatory checks: block until completed.
