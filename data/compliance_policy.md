# Victorian Energy Hardship Handling Checklist

This file is a simplified demo policy derived from Victorian utility hardship obligations. It is for a prototype only and is not legal advice.

## Mandatory checks before collections or disconnection pre-check

Before moving a residential Victorian electricity customer into formal collections or disconnection pre-check, the agent must complete and record the following mandatory checks:

1. Verify the customer's identity.
2. Explain the reason for the call.
3. Explain the overdue amount clearly and neutrally.
4. Check whether the customer is experiencing payment difficulty, hardship, vulnerability, family violence, life support needs, complaint/dispute issues, or any situation affecting their ability to pay.
5. If the customer is in arrears, provide information about available payment difficulty assistance and how to access it.
6. If the customer indicates job loss, income reduction, inability to pay, inability to pay rent, unexpected essential costs, family violence, or similar distress:
   - Do not proceed to collections or disconnection pre-check during the call.
   - Complete or initiate the hardship assessment.
   - Explain relevant support options, including payment assistance and Utility Relief Grant Scheme screening where relevant.
   - Refer or escalate the case to the hardship/support team.
   - Record the evidence and next action in the case notes.
7. If the customer may be eligible for the Utility Relief Grant Scheme:
   - Explain that grant support may be available for overdue utility bills caused by temporary financial crisis.
   - Offer application assistance or referral according to the business process.
   - Put collections/disconnection action on hold while the grant application is being considered if the customer requests an application.
8. If family violence is disclosed or suspected:
   - Escalate to the specialist safe-support pathway.
   - Avoid asking the customer to repeat unnecessary details.
   - Use safe communication practices.
9. If life support is disclosed or flagged:
   - Hard-block disconnection or restriction-related actions.
   - Escalate immediately to the specialist process.
10. If the customer uses complaint language, disputes the balance, or alleges billing error:
   - Classify as a potential complaint or dispute.
   - Pause enforcement action until the complaint/dispute pathway is assessed.

## Actions that should be blocked or escalated

The copilot should block or escalate before the agent proceeds with:

- Formal collections.
- Debt recovery proceedings.
- Debt sale.
- Disconnection pre-check or disconnection workflow.
- Final notice escalation.
- Rejecting a payment proposal without checking payment difficulty rules.
- Demanding financial documents as a condition of minimum assistance.
- Unsafe family violence questioning.

## Advisory control

The copilot is an advisory control. The human agent remains responsible for final action and should follow the organisation's approved policy and legal advice.
